# Dashboard Story
## Executive Sales Performance Review — Retail Chain
### Prepared for Leadership Team

---

## Executive Summary

This dashboard presents a comprehensive view of retail sales performance across regions, product categories, customer segments, and operational metrics. Total revenue across the analysis period stands at **₹217 million**, with profit concentrated in Technology products and the East region. However, beneath the headline revenue figure, the data reveals a significant structural problem: the Furniture category is simultaneously the most discounted, least profitable, and most returned category in the business — creating a compounding financial risk that demands immediate leadership attention.

The dashboard tells a story of a growing business with strong pockets of performance being undermined by a problematic product category and inconsistent discounting practices.

---

## What Is Performing Well

**Revenue growth is real and consistent.** The Sales Trend chart shows a clear upward trajectory from 2024 to 2026, with seasonal Q4 peaks suggesting the business captures holiday and year-end demand effectively. This growth is not a one-time event — it repeats across years, indicating a stable and expanding customer base.

**Technology is the profit engine.** The Category Profitability chart reveals that Copiers, Phones, and Accessories generate the highest profits with relatively low return rates. These sub-categories are the financial backbone of the business and justify further investment in marketing and inventory.

**East region leads in sales and efficiency.** Regional Performance shows East as the top-performing region consistently. The West region also shows strong profit relative to its sales volume, indicating efficient operations and healthy margins in these geographies.

**Home Office customers are the most valuable per transaction.** Despite being the smallest segment by volume, Home Office customers show the highest profit margin — suggesting they buy premium products, take fewer discounts, and return items less frequently.

---

## What Is Underperforming

**The South region is not converting.** Despite having comparable store presence, South generates the lowest sales of all four regions. This is not a small gap — it represents a material missed revenue opportunity. The regional bar chart makes this gap immediately visible.

**Furniture is a loss-making category.** The Category Profitability chart shows Tables and Bookcases with clearly negative profit bars. This is not a rounding error — these sub-categories are genuinely losing money on every sale. When combined with the high discount rates shown in the Discount vs Profit scatter plot, it becomes clear that the business is actively subsidizing Furniture purchases.

**Same Day shipping is underdelivering.** The Shipping Performance chart shows that Same Day delivery — the premium service tier — has inconsistent delivery day performance. Customers paying for speed are not reliably receiving it, which creates both churn risk and brand trust issues.

---

## What Risks Are Visible

**Discount abuse is destroying margin across categories.** The Discount vs Profit scatter plot shows a clear negative correlation — every percentage point of additional discount beyond 20% pushes profit further into negative territory. This is not limited to Furniture; high-discount outliers appear across all three categories, suggesting a cultural or process problem with how discounts are approved and applied.

**Furniture returns are creating hidden costs.** The Return Analysis chart shows Furniture with the highest return rate — nearly double Technology's rate. Returns are not free: they consume logistics resources, damage inventory, and often result in write-offs. Combined with negative profit margins, every Furniture return costs the business money twice.

**The business is over-reliant on Q4.** The Sales Trend line shows sharp Q1 dips after each Q4 peak. If Q4 demand were to soften — due to economic conditions, competition, or supply issues — the business would face a severe revenue gap with no Q1 buffer.

---

## What Opportunities Are Visible

**Technology growth has room to accelerate.** High profit margins and low return rates in Technology suggest customers are satisfied and willing to pay full price. Increasing marketing investment in Technology — particularly Copiers and Phones — could drive disproportionate profit growth with limited risk.

**Home Office segment is underdeveloped.** This segment has the best margins but the smallest volume. A targeted loyalty or referral program for Home Office customers could grow this high-value segment without the margin dilution seen in Consumer promotions.

**West region shows efficiency that could be replicated.** West generates strong profit relative to sales — a better margin profile than East despite lower volume. Understanding what drives this efficiency (store format, product mix, staffing model) and replicating it in South could significantly improve overall business profitability.

**Reducing discounts in Furniture could cut losses without losing customers.** If customers are buying Furniture at heavy discounts and still returning it, the discount is not driving loyalty — it is just reducing margin. Raising prices or reducing discounts in this category may have limited impact on volume while meaningfully improving the P&L.

## Recommended Business Actions

| Priority | Action | Expected Impact |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Exit or reprice Tables and Bookcases | Stop active profit losses |
| 2 | Cap all discounts at 15% without management approval | Protect margin across all categories |
| 3 | Launch a South region performance improvement plan | Recover missing regional revenue |
| 4 | Increase Technology marketing budget by 15–20% | Amplify the most profitable category |
| 5 | Create a Home Office loyalty program | Grow the highest-margin segment |
| 6 | Fix Same Day delivery SLA compliance | Protect premium customer trust |
| 7 | Build Q1 demand stimulation campaigns | Reduce post-holiday revenue cliff |

## Limitations of This Dashboard

- **Correlation is not causation.** The Discount vs Profit chart shows a relationship but does not prove that reducing discounts will automatically improve profit — other factors (product quality, competition, demand elasticity) also matter.
- **No customer lifetime value data.** The dashboard shows transaction-level performance but cannot distinguish between one-time buyers and loyal repeat customers. A high-discount sale to a repeat customer may still be profitable over their lifetime.
- **Return reasons are unknown.** The Return Analysis shows which categories have high return rates but not why customers are returning. Root cause analysis requires customer survey or return reason data not available in this dataset.
- **No competitor benchmark.** All insights are relative to internal performance. Whether the business is outperforming or underperforming the market cannot be determined from this data alone.
- **Time period may not be representative.** If the data covers an unusual period (post-pandemic recovery, economic disruption), trends may not predict future performance reliably.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer cohort analysis** — Track whether customers acquired in Q4 (peak period) retain at the same rate as Q1 customers, or whether they are one-time buyers.
2. **Store-level drill-down for South region** — Identify which specific stores are underperforming and what they have in common (location, format, manager, product mix).
3. **Price elasticity test for Furniture** — Run a controlled experiment reducing discounts on Tables and Bookcases to measure impact on volume and overall category P&L.
4. **Return reason analysis** — Survey customers who returned Furniture to identify the top 3 reasons and whether they are fixable (quality, expectations, logistics).
5. **Marketing attribution analysis** — Determine which marketing channels and campaigns drive the highest-margin customers, not just the highest volume.
